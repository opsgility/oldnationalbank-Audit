# Pre-Work — Before the Copilot in Excel for Audit Session

**Do this the day before the session. It takes about 10 minutes.**

The session is hands-on. Every exercise uses synthetic Old National Bank-style sample workbooks, and Copilot in Excel only works on files saved in your Microsoft 365 environment **with AutoSave on**. To follow along, the sample workbooks must be in **your OneDrive** (or a shared SharePoint location the instructor provides) before we start.

---

## Why OneDrive matters (more than it did in Word)

Copilot in Excel **requires** the file to be cloud-saved with AutoSave on — it will not run on a local-only workbook. Cloud-saved files also give you:

- **Version history** — Copilot edits cells directly and auto-saves; version history is how you roll back (used in every exercise).
- **Refreshable imports** — Power Query connections to other workbooks (desktop feature, demoed in Exercise 2).
- **Real-time co-authoring** — multiple auditors in the same workpaper.

## Step 1 — Confirm you have access

If anything below is missing, contact your IT/Copilot administrator **before** the session.

- [ ] You can sign in to Microsoft 365 (Office.com) with your ONB account.
- [ ] You have a **Microsoft 365 Copilot** license (open Excel → look for the **Copilot** button on the Home ribbon).
- [ ] You can open **OneDrive** from Office.com and see your files.
- [ ] You can open a workbook in **Excel for the web** (our classroom baseline).

## Step 2 — Create a folder in OneDrive

1. Go to **office.com** and sign in.
2. Open **OneDrive**.
3. Click **+ Add new → Folder**.
4. Name it: **Copilot Audit Session**.

## Step 3 — Upload the sample workbooks

Upload **all** of the following into your **Copilot Audit Session** folder. Your instructor will provide these files (or a link).

| # | File | Used in |
|---|------|---------|
| 1 | RCSA-Control-Matrix.xlsx | Exercise 1, 5 |
| 2 | GL-Subledger-Reconciliation.xlsx | Exercise 2 |
| 3 | Wire-Transfer-Population.xlsx | Exercise 3 |
| 4 | Audit-Hours-Budget-vs-Actual.xlsx | Exercise 4 |

**To upload:** open the folder → **Upload → Files** → select the workbooks → wait for the green check on each.

> **Important:** These workbooks contain **synthetic data only**. Never upload live audit workpapers, customer data, or anything confidential to a training exercise.

## Step 4 — Quick readiness check (2 minutes)

1. In OneDrive, open **Wire-Transfer-Population.xlsx** in **Excel for the web**.
2. Confirm **AutoSave** is on (top-left toggle).
3. Click the **Copilot** button on the Home ribbon.
4. Note the toggle above the prompt box: **Chat only** vs. **Allow editing** — we use both in the session.
5. In Chat-only mode, type: `How many rows of data are on this sheet, and what are the column headers?`
6. If you get an answer, you're ready. If the Copilot button is missing or greyed out, contact your administrator before the session.

## Step 5 — One habit to bring

Before the session, find one **real formula** in a workbook you inherited and didn't write. In Exercise 2 you'll learn to have Copilot explain formulas like it — bring the curiosity, not the file.
